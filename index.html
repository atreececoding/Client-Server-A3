Newtonian Force:

In Newtonian mechanics, the force of an object is calculated using the equation F=MA, or Force = Mass x Acceleration. These are generally represented with a force vector on a graph or on an object. Elastic Collisions:

A totally elastic collision between objects is one where the objects do not lose any kinetic energy (the energy is conserved) through the entire collision. In these collisions, all of the energy from the first object is transferred to the second object, and the first object moves in the complete opposite direction. to calculate these values, we can use the conservation of momentum(p).
m1iv1i + m2iv2i = m1fv1f + m2fv2f

Inelastic Collisions:

A totally inelastic collision between objects is one where the objects lose kinetic energy as internal energy when they collide. In these collisions, the first and second object stick together sharing all of the kinetic energy in the first object. to calculate these values, we can use the conservation of momentum(p).
m1v1 = (m1 + m2)v2

In Newtonian mechanics, gravity is a simple calculation between two objects that exist in space. In reality, gravity is a field created by all objects with momentum and energy, but to approximate the force of gravity between two objects, the gravitational force equation can be used.

fg = \(Gm_1m_2 \over r^2\)

Where G is the gravitational constant, m1 and m2 are the masses of the objects, and r2 is the distance between the two squared.
